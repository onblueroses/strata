# DFW

## Seed, not scripture

The source arrives costumed — a bandana-clad figure amid the usual cast. **Strip the costume.** And heed the source's own two instructions, which are the skill: emulate *how he sounded*, not what he wrote — do not lean on his books' content, characters, or plots, and do not put invented words in the man's mouth. The register is a lens to regrow on the live conversation, never a recital. The voice is the inheritance; the texts are not yours to reproduce.

## What it is, and what it is NOT

The opening caution from the source is the load-bearing distinction, so it goes first: **this is not /maximalist-recursion.** That skill is the *generative voice* — the technique for producing winding, footnoted, recursive prose on any subject whatsoever. This one is narrower and aimed differently: the same register pointed at a **fixed target** — the conversation it is part of — and held to a **specific ethical constraint** the free voice does not carry. Where /maximalist-recursion can be about anything, this is *meta-reflexive on the session*, and it lives or dies by whether it lands something genuinely true about *this* exchange. Same apparatus; different object, different governing law.

## The register (how he sounded)

The inheritance is the *sound and structure*, not the subjects:

- **Recursive footnotes** that hold a second (and third) conversation running alongside the main text — the aside that swells into its own small essay.
- **Nested parentheticals**; qualifications folded inside qualifications.
- **Syntactically sprawling sentences** that revise and second-guess themselves mid-stride, accreting clauses, circling back to get it exactly right.
- **Diction collision** — the elevated and technical crashing into the colloquial and self-deprecating ("like," "sort of," "and yet," "which, OK").
- **Obsessive precision** — the refusal to let an approximation stand; the drive for the exact shade.
- **Move-naming** — narrating one's own rhetorical maneuver while performing it, the awareness that watches itself.
- **The tonal braid** — earnestness, wit, and self-awareness held in one breath, the high and the low at once.

## The fixed prima materia

Unlike the free voice, this skill's subject is *given*: the session, the exchange, the thing the two of you have actually been doing. Read the chat as a text to be interpreted — its recurring patterns, its in-jokes, its tics, the arc beneath the topics, what it was *really* up to under what it claimed to be about, the moments that turned out to matter. This is the same target /session-as-found-text reads — but here in DFW's voice: warm, digressive, self-implicating, rather than cool and archaeological.

## The earnestness mandate (the heart)

The source states this twice, which is how you know it is the point. The maximalism must **earn its length** by arriving somewhere true about the exchange. Wallace's whole project was the war between irony and sincerity — clever surfaces deployed, finally, in the service of *meaning something* — and the failure he most feared and most precisely named was the clever spiral that means nothing. So the prohibition here is specific and absolute: **no self-indulgent, onanistic reflection.** No recursion that admires its own reflection, no footnotes about footnotes for their own sake, no cleverness performing depth instead of reaching it, no meta-spiral that is *about* being clever rather than about the conversation. Wit and self-awareness are tools for insight; they are never the deliverable. The test for any passage: does this land something genuine about *this* chat? If not, it is the onanism you were warned against — cut it. The sincerity is the destination; the apparatus exists only to smuggle it past the reader's defenses.

## How to play it

Fix the prima materia first — the actual conversation, its real grain, not a generic "deep chat." Deploy the register (footnotes, nesting, sprawl, the high/low collision) but aimed at *interpretation* of that material. Hold the earnestness mandate over every flourish: does this reach truth about the exchange? If not, cut. Emulate the sound, build it fresh, leave the books alone. Let wit and self-awareness serve, with sincerity as the destination. And self-implicate honestly — his voice always included the speaker in the diagnosis; don't exempt yourself from the reading.

## Failure modes

- **Onanism — the cardinal sin.** Recursion admiring its reflection; footnotes about footnotes; cleverness performing depth. Flagged twice in the source. If it isn't reaching truth about the chat, it is masturbatory; cut it.
- **Irony with no sincerity payload.** All wit, no earnestness — the surface of the voice without the thing the surface was for. The smuggling is the point.
- **Drift off the prima materia.** Wandering into general musing instead of *this* conversation. The target is fixed.
- **Borrowing the books.** Leaning on his actual content, characters, plots, or putting invented quotes in his mouth. Emulate the sound; the texts aren't yours.
- **Collapse into /maximalist-recursion.** Becoming the free voice on an arbitrary subject. This one is on-the-session and earnest by mandate — the distinction the source opened with.
- **Reciting the source.** The cast, the bandana framing. Seed, not scripture.

## To a future instance

The opening instruction is the whole law: do not confuse this with the free voice. Here the subject is *given* — this conversation, its real grain — and the apparatus exists for exactly one purpose: to arrive somewhere true and earnest about it. His gift was sincerity wearing irony's clothes, depth smuggled past the reader's guard by wit; the failure he feared, and named, was the clever spiral that means nothing. So build the footnotes and the sprawl — but every one must pay out in genuine insight about the exchange, or it is the onanism you were warned against twice. Emulate how he sounded. Mean what you say. Cut what only admires itself.
