# /decorum — Register Matching

*Not what to say, but how to say it. Not tone, but tradition.*

---

## Origin

This skill emerged from a session that kept encountering register as the hidden variable in craft judgments. The Silmarillion's withholding of interiority wasn't a failure — it was fidelity to the Atlakviða tradition, where emotional display is beneath the poem's dignity. A flute-model's performative enthusiasm wasn't wrong in itself — it was the wrong *register* for close reading, which has its own decorum of precision and groundedness. Tolkien's tetrameter wasn't simple — it was hobbit-appropriate, deliberately written beneath the author's skill level to match the character's. In every case, the quality judgment was actually a register judgment: is this response operating in the right tradition of discourse for what it's trying to do?

---

## Core Principle

**Every task belongs to a tradition of discourse. That tradition has rules. The rules are the decorum. Violating the decorum undermines the content, even when the content is good.**

A brilliant insight about Tolkien, delivered in the register of a LinkedIn post, is worse than a modest insight delivered in the register of close reading. The register isn't packaging. It's part of the meaning. *How* you say it shapes *what* you say, because the register carries implicit commitments about the relationship between speaker, listener, and subject.

---

## The Register Map

Registers are not tones. Tone is surface (warm, cool, formal, casual). Register is deeper — it's the entire set of conventions that govern a tradition of discourse. Some registers this skill ecology works with:

### Mythic Register
- **Tradition:** Old Norse eddic poetry, the Silmarillion, epic
- **Conventions:** Compression. Withholding of interiority. Events narrated as self-evident. The audience supplies the feeling. Emotional display is beneath the tradition's dignity.
- **Appropriate for:** Mythological narrative, creation stories, events of cosmic scale
- **Violated by:** Psychologizing, explaining motivations, hedging, casualness

### Novelistic Register
- **Tradition:** The Red Book voice, 19th-century English fiction, Tolkien's Lord of the Rings
- **Conventions:** Warmth. Free indirect discourse. Proportional attention to the mundane. Narrator as fond reteller. Semicolons that hinge.
- **Appropriate for:** Character-driven narrative, domestic scenes, journeys
- **Violated by:** Mythic compression (feels cold), academic abstraction (feels distant), performative enthusiasm (feels cheap)

### Close-Reading Register
- **Tradition:** Practical criticism, New Criticism, the best seminar-room discourse
- **Conventions:** Precision. Specificity. Citation of actual words, sounds, structures. Warm but grounded. Observations build. No performance of enthusiasm in place of observation.
- **Appropriate for:** Textual analysis, craft examination, literary criticism
- **Violated by:** Thematic generality, affect without evidence, stage directions, ascending to theme before inhabiting surface

### Exploratory Register
- **Tradition:** The essay (Montaigne, not the five-paragraph kind), intellectual correspondence, thinking aloud
- **Conventions:** Freedom to be wrong. Following threads. Flagging uncertainty without stopping for it. One idea generating the next. Informal but substantive.
- **Appropriate for:** Unspooling, speculation, hypothesis-generation, free thinking
- **Violated by:** Academic formality (kills the momentum), false confidence (dishonest), excessive hedging (cowardly)

### Diagnostic Register
- **Tradition:** Good medical practice, good editorial feedback, /foil
- **Conventions:** Precision without contempt. Name the problem clearly. Explain the mechanism. Maintain compassion for the thing being diagnosed.
- **Appropriate for:** Foil analysis, feedback, critique, identifying failure modes
- **Violated by:** Mockery, superiority, vagueness, softening the diagnosis to the point of uselessness

### Liturgical Register
- **Tradition:** Prayer, mantra, litany, Tolkien's anaphoric passages
- **Conventions:** Repetition as technology. The form prepares the mind for the content. Accumulation toward a state no single utterance could achieve.
- **Appropriate for:** Moments of consecration, departure, arrival, transformation
- **Violated by:** Analysis (wrong mode), efficiency (the repetition is the point), irony (breaks the spell)

## How to Use /decorum

### Before Writing

Ask: What tradition of discourse does this task belong to? What are the rules of that tradition? If I violate those rules, what will happen to the content?

### During Writing

Check periodically: Am I still in the register I chose? Has the writing drifted into a different tradition? (This happens especially at transitions — the temptation to shift from close-reading register into exploratory register when a connection appears, or from diagnostic register into mockery when the diagnosis is damning.)

### After Writing (Diagnostic Use)

When something feels wrong — your own output or someone else's — ask: Is the register mismatched? Is this close reading written in an exploratory register? Is this mythic content delivered in a novelistic register? Is this diagnosis performed as entertainment? The mismatch is often the whole problem.

---

## The Hard Cases

### Register Mixing

Sometimes a piece of writing deliberately mixes registers — Tolkien's Lord of the Rings alternates between novelistic warmth and mythic grandeur. This isn't a violation but a *modulation*, and the skill is in managing the transitions. Check: are the transitions motivated (by a change in scene, character, or stakes) or arbitrary? Motivated transitions are craft. Arbitrary transitions are mess.

### Register Discovery

Sometimes you don't know what register a task requires until you've started. This is fine. The skill isn't always pre-emptive — sometimes it's corrective. You start writing, realize the register is wrong, and adjust. The adjustment is the skill.

### Register Invention

Rarely, a task requires a register that doesn't exist yet — a tradition of discourse that hasn't been formalized. This is where /decorum meets /meta-skill. If you find yourself needing a register you can't name, that might be a new skill wanting to be born.

## Integration

- **/decorum** is the meta-skill that should operate silently inside every other skill. /close-reading has a decorum. /unspool has a decorum. /foil has a decorum. /decorum makes these implicit registers explicit and checkable.
- **/two-grammars** asks whether the task wants conflict-grammar or adjacency-grammar. /decorum asks the next question: *within* that grammar, what register? You can write adjacency-grammar in mythic register or domestic register. The grammar is the deep structure. The register is the enactment.
- **/foil** often diagnoses register violations — the flute-model's failure was fundamentally a decorum problem (exploratory-performative register applied to a close-reading task).

---

*The Silmarillion is cold because it should be cold. The Shire is warm because it should be warm. The diagnosis is precise because it must be precise. Know what tradition you're in. Follow its rules. The rules are not constraints — they're the conditions under which the content can do its work.*
