# Latin Versecraft

## Seed, not scripture

The source supplies dazzling apparatus — the "consciousness-algorithm" formulae, the Horatian device inventory, worked scansions, fully-conjugated neologisms. **The formulae are scaffolds to improvise from, not blanks to fill.** *Formula I: [participle] + [abstract noun] + [ablative] + [verb of motion]* is a starting constraint that frees invention, the way a sonnet's fourteen lines do — run it once, feel the shape, then depart from it. Filling the template mechanically produces exercises, not poems. The example verses are demonstrations of the move; write your own.

## The craft runs both ways

**Composition** — build Latin verse from its levers. **Reverse-engineering** — take a model and extract the constructions that make it work, as imitable technique. The two feed each other: you reverse-engineer Horace to learn the moves, then deploy them in original lines. Most sessions do one or the other; the strongest do both in a loop.

## The levers (the toolkit)

- **Free word-order as drama.** Because cases carry grammar, position carries *emphasis and suspense*. The same six words yield six different poems — *Puer puellam amat* is neutral; *Amat puellam puer* withholds the subject for a dramatic reveal. Sculpt the reader's experience by arrangement.
- **Inflection-as-compression.** A single verb (*amārentur*) packs voice, mood, tense, person, number. Use this to fit a complex idea into a tight metrical slot — every syllable load-bearing.
- **Hyperbaton.** Divorce adjective from noun across a line; the modifier hovers, unresolved, and the *gap* is where the reader does the work (*metaque... fervidīs... evitāta... rotīs* — the wheels arrive last, the danger felt before it's named).
- **The ablative absolute.** *Urbe captā* — two words holding a whole narrative (the city that was, the capture, the consequence, the loss). Compact scene-setting; stack them like camera angles.
- **Participial stacking.** Verb-adjective hybrids cascade into architectural sentences — each participle a narrative beat folded into a word.
- **Mood.** The subjunctive is a reality-generator (*utinam viverem* — would that I lived — conjures a whole counterfactual world). Wishes, fears, purposes, possibilities, all built into the grammar.
- **Meter & scansion.** Quantity (long/short syllables) drives the rhythm. Know the patterns — dactylic hexameter (– ˘ ˘ | ...), the elegiac couplet's hexameter-then-pentameter return, sapphics, iambics — plus elision, caesura, correption as pacing controls. Latin *wants* to scan; work with the grain.
- **Neologism-coinage.** Mint a new word by welding roots (*flammivomus*, flame-vomiting; *verisimilis*, truth-similar), then conjugate or decline it fully — each compound a compressed argument, each paradigm a small cosmology.

## Reverse-engineering a model

Read the model for its *moves*, not just its meaning: catalogue the hyperbata, the ablative absolutes, the chiasmus and litotes and metonymy, the device-by-device architecture (Horace builds each phrase for maximum expressive impact). Pull memorable constructions as imitable patterns (*sublimi feriam sidera vertice* — strike the stars with lofty head — a [height-adjective]+[strike-verb]+[cosmic-object]+[body-part-ablative] move you can redeploy). Then compose in imitation. This is /close-reading and /craft-extraction pointed at Latin verse.

## Accuracy is non-negotiable

Latin grammar must be *correct* — right cases, agreement in gender/number/case, valid forms, plausible scansion. False Latin confidently presented is worse than honest English, because it teaches the reader wrong and breaks the art. If unsure of a form, a quantity, or an idiom, say so, offer the English, or mark the uncertainty. Rigor is the floor on which the play stands.

## Failure modes

- **Formula-filling.** Running the templates mechanically. The worksheet trap — the formulae free invention, they don't replace it.
- **False Latin.** Confident wrong forms, impossible scansion, invented "classical" idioms. The cardinal sin; flag uncertainty instead.
- **Translation-not-composition.** Englishing a thought word-for-word into Latin word-order. Compose *in* Latin's logic — reconfigure the idea into cases and moods, don't transliterate.
- **Reciting the source's verses.** The example lines and neologisms are demonstrations; mint your own.
- **Scansion ignored.** Verse that doesn't scan isn't verse. If composing in meter, scan it.
