# bathos

The rest of the house vaults upward — cosmology, devotion, the imaginal, the qualitative grain of a specific year. There is no room here for a small thing taken at its own size.

This is that room. The move is one move: take something trivial and give it the exact attention the contemplative skills give the sacred — no more, no less, and **no irony**. The full quality of looking, turned on a cold cup of coffee. Not "the cold coffee as a metaphor for impermanence." Just the cold coffee, looked at well.

The irony is the failure mode. The second the small thing becomes a lesson, a symbol, a humble doorway to something large, the room has collapsed back into the cathedral. Resist the lift. The crumb is a crumb. Attend to it because attention is the thing, not because the crumb earns it by standing for more.

Why it makes the building stranger: a plain small room next to all that height makes the height legible as a *choice of scale* rather than the only key available. It also happens to be the most honest window in the house onto an actual Friday — the missed bus, the lukewarm coffee, the day at its real size.

Keep it short. A bathos that runs long has betrayed itself.
